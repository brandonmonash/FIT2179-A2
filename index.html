Australia's Atmosphere

CO₂ Levels Are Rising
And Not Stabilizing

The following graphs show the rising levels of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, its growth rate and its standard deviation. These graphs demonstrate the alarming increase in CO₂ levels over time, which is a major contributor to global warming and climate change. The data clearly indicates that CO₂ levels are not stabilizing but rather continuing to rise, posing significant challenges for environmental sustainability and climate mitigation efforts.

This graph represents the present atmospheric carbon dioxide concentration that is measured in Parts Per Million (PPM). The area chart demonstrates a clear and upward trend in CO₂ levels throughout the recorded period (1976-2024) without any decrease. The dots in the graph represent the year that the data was recorded with its own Carbon dioxide levels in that year. This data is from CSIRO AU (https://data.csiro.au/collection/csiro:63718).
The data here shows the standard deviation of the carbon dioxide concentration in parts per million (PPM). The data is calculated by the standard deviation of the concentration during that time period with the red dots being the data that was recorded when it was above the threshold of 0.3 which is considered to be concerning standard deviation. Data is from CSIRO AU (https://data.csiro.au/collection/csiro:63718).
GR(ppm/year) shows the growth rate of carbon dioxide concentration in parts per million every year. It is shown in a bar graph which also indicates the amount of increase that happened within that year which is in a compounding increase. Data is from CSIRO AU (https://data.csiro.au/collection/csiro:63718).
